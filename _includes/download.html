COVID-19 Country Grouping

This project employs a simple K-means clustering algorithm, along with tools like IBM SPSS Modeler and JMP to evaluate COVID-19 risk levels across countries. It analyses factors such as demographics, GDP and disease rates while managing multicollinearity to ensure the validity of the clustering outcomes. The simple K-means method effectively groups nations by COVID-19 risk offering a benchmark for comparison. This facilitates a mutual learning process allowing governments to learn from each other and adapt policies to their development status and specific COVID-19 risks.

Patient Survey Experience

This project integrates Python, SQL and R to streamline data preparation, focusing on data merging and cleansing. It utilises surveys to assess patient satisfaction through a Linear Model. Data is transferred from Excel to MySQL, forming precise database tables. SQL further refines these tables to ensure data consistency. R analyses this data, applying Linear Regression to predict satisfaction and identify influential variables. Finally, a performance impact chart is created to highlight areas for improvement, suggesting ward adjustments to enhance patient satisfaction.

Credit Card Billable Prediction

This project conducted an in-depth analysis of a credit facility dataset, uncovering insights into customer demographics, credit behaviour and financial trends through various visualisation techniques. It then developed a multiple linear regression model to accurately predict billable amounts, demonstrating its reliability in extrapolating to new data. Through predictive modelling, the project provided actionable insights that aid in resource allocation, risk assessment and strategic planning, thereby enhancing the efficiency and performance of credit facilities.

Loan Status Prediction

This project employs an Artificial Neural Network to predict customer default risks within a dataset of credit facilities. It involves data processing in Python, addressing missing values and encoding categorical variables. Exploratory Data Analysis techniques are utilised to understand data distributions and identify outliers. The model’s performance is assessed using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score and AUC-ROC curve analysis. Additionally, the importance of predictors is evaluated to interpret the model’s decision-making process, thereby contributing to improved risk assessment strategies.

Civil Conflict And Food Aid

This project analyses governance, food aid and conflict dynamics in Sub-Saharan Africa using data analysis and visualisation. It explores correlations among governance indicators, economic factors and humanitarian assistance, revealing how autocratic governance, limited economic growth and uneven aid distribution worsen conflicts. Emphasising the complexity of aid distribution's impact on conflict escalation, the project aims to deepen understanding and propose strategies for mitigating conflicts and fostering sustainable development in the region.
